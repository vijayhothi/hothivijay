The **DISPLACE 2024 Challenge** evaluates speaker diarization systems on far-field, multilingual conversational speech recorded in unconstrained, real-world environments. This paper describes our submitted adaptive multi-scale diarization system, which addresses key challenges:

- **Overlapping speech** in natural conversations
- **Background noise and reverberation** in far-field recordings
- **Multi-lingual and code-switching** scenarios

The system combines multi-scale temporal feature extraction with adaptive speaker embedding clustering, achieving competitive results on the DISPLACE 2024 benchmark and demonstrating the effectiveness of adaptive architectures for robust diarization.

Presented at the **11th International Conference on Pattern Recognition and Machine Intelligence (PReMI 2025)**, published in Springer Lecture Notes in Computer Science.
